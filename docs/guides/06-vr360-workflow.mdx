## What is VR360 Mono?

**VR360 Mono** captures a full 360√ó180¬∞ sphere around the camera in a single (monoscopic) view. Unlike VR180, there's no stereoscopic depth, but viewers can look in **any direction** including behind and above/below.

**Key Characteristics:**
- **360¬∞ horizontal √ó 180¬∞ vertical** coverage
- **Monoscopic** (single image, no stereo depth)
- **Equirectangular** projection (like a world map)
- **2:1 aspect ratio** (e.g., 5120√ó2560)
- **Full freedom** of rotation

**Use Cases:**
- YouTube 360 videos
- Virtual tours
- Location scouting
- Event coverage
- Educational content
- Real estate walkthroughs

**Advantages:**
- Full 360¬∞ coverage (look anywhere!)
- Simpler than stereoscopic
- Smaller file sizes than VR180
- Easier to compose (spherical thinking)

**Limitations:**
- No depth perception (monoscopic)
- Can't hide anything (camera sees everything!)
- Viewer controls orientation (no camera rotation)

---

## VR360 vs VR180

| Feature | VR360 Mono | VR180 Stereo |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Coverage** | 360¬∞ sphere | 180¬∞ front hemisphere |
| **Depth** | No (monoscopic) | Yes (stereoscopic) |
| **Cameras** | 1 | 2 (left/right) |
| **Best For** | Tours, locations | Narrative, seated experiences |
| **File Size** | Smaller | Larger |
| **Viewer Freedom** | Complete | Front-facing only |

## Quick Start (15 Minutes)

### Prerequisites

- Blender 4.0+
- PE Camera Rigs addon installed

   - **Quality:** Preview / Production

4. **Configure STEP 1 Settings:**
   - **Lighting:** Choose setup

   - **‚òë Include Reference Sphere** (for testing)

5. Click **`[1Ô∏è‚É£ Create VR360 Scene]`**

**Result:**
- `VR360_Camera` with panoramic settings
- Lighting (if enabled)
- Cyclorama (if enabled)

---

### Step 2: Compose Your 360¬∞ Scene (Variable)

**Critical 360¬∞ Thinking:**
- Camera sees **everything** (including behind, above, below)
- No "off-camera" areas
- Think spherically!

**Camera Positioning:**
1. Position `VR360_Camera` at viewer's eye height
   - Standing: 1.6-1.8m Z
   - Sitting: 1.0-1.2m Z
2. Camera represents viewer's head position
3. Face camera toward main content (optional - viewer can look anywhere)

**Scene Composition:**
- **Front (0¬∞):** Primary content
- **Sides (¬±90¬∞):** Secondary interest
- **Back (180¬∞):** Don't leave empty - add context!
- **Ceiling:** Consider overhead details
- **Floor:** Ground textures, shadows

**What to Hide:**
- Tripods, stands, equipment
- Lights (unless intentionally visible)
- Cameras, rigs
- Unfinished areas

**Tip:** Add interesting details in all directions. Empty space breaks immersion!

### Step 3: Render 360¬∞ Sequence (Hours - Variable)

1. **Save your .blend file**
2. Set **Frame Start** and **Frame End**
3. In **STEP 2**, click **`[2Ô∏è‚É£ Render EXR Sequence]`**

**What Happens:**
- Renders full 360¬∞ panorama for each frame

- Equirectangular projection (2:1)
- Monoscopic (one camera only)

**Time Estimate:**
- Preview: 1-3 min/frame
- Production: 5-15 min/frame
- Depends on scene complexity

**Crash Safety:**
- Resume anytime (skips completed frames)
- Check output folder for progress
- EXR files preserve all data

1. Wait for Step 2

**To View/Edit:**
- Switch to `VR360_Compositor` scene
- Open **Compositing** workspace
- Adjust nodes as needed

### Step 5: Render Final Video (Hours)

1. In **STEP 4**, click **`[4Ô∏è‚É£ Render YouTube Video]`**

**What Happens:**
- Renders compositor output
- Outputs equirectangular video
- H.264 codec (YouTube-compatible)

## 360¬∞ Scene Composition

### Directional Zones

Think of your scene as a sphere divided into zones:

**Front Hemisphere (0-90¬∞)**
- Primary content
- Main action
- Where most viewers look first

**Side Zones (90-180¬∞ left/right)**
- Secondary content
- Contextual elements
- Peripheral interest

**Back Hemisphere (180-270¬∞)**
- Background context
- Environment detail
- Often overlooked but important!

**Vertical (Above/Below)**
- Ceiling details
- Sky/environment
- Floor/ground

---

### Depth Layers

Even without stereo depth, use compositional depth:

**Foreground (2-5m)**
- Nearest interesting elements
- Frame the view
- Add context

**Midground (5-15m)**
- Main subjects
- Primary action
- Focal point

**Background (15m+)**
- Environment
- Atmosphere
- Horizon/sky

---

### Best Practices

**Do:**
- Add interest in all 360¬∞
- Consider ceiling and floor
- Use environmental storytelling
- Hide equipment
- Think about viewer's natural gaze direction

**Don't:**
- Leave any direction completely empty
- Put important content only in one direction
- Forget about what's above/below
- Show cameras, lights, tripods

## Camera Movement in VR360

### General Rules

**Minimize camera movement!**
- Static cameras work best
- Viewer controls orientation (no camera rotation!)
- Movement can cause motion sickness

### Safe Movements

**Dollies (Forward/Backward)**
- Slow, smooth movement
- Constant speed
- Use for virtual tours

**Height Changes**
- Gradual vertical movement
- Simulate climbing stairs
- Elevator rides

**What to NEVER Do:**
- **Rotate camera** (viewer controls rotation!)
- Fast pans
- Shaky movements
- Sudden starts/stops

### If You Must Move

**Guidelines:**
- Maximum speed: Walk pace
- Ease in/out (bezier curves)
- Warn viewers (title card)
- Test on VR headset first

## Lighting for 360¬∞

### Challenges

**360¬∞ coverage means:**
- Light sources visible from some angles
- No "off-camera" lights
- Shadows appear everywhere

### Solutions

**HDRI Environment**
- Natural 360¬∞ lighting
- Consistent from all angles
- Realistic reflections

**Distributed Lighting**
- Many small area lights
- Evenly distributed
- Soft, ambient feel

**Strategic Placement**
- Place lights where they make sense (ceiling lamps, windows)
- Make lights part of the scene
- Use practicals (visible light sources)

## Step-by-Step Tutorial

### Tutorial 1: Simple 360¬∞ Scene

**Goal:** Create basic 360¬∞ VR video

**Setup:**
1. Create a room or environment
2. Add objects around 360¬∞

**Workflow:**
1. **Step 1:** Create VR360 Scene

## Advanced Techniques

### Text/Graphics in 360¬∞

**Placement:**
- **Top/Bottom:** Always visible (viewer can't avoid)
- **Center (0¬∞ horizontal):** Natural forward position
- **Sides:** Requires head turn

**Best Practices:**
- Keep titles at top or bottom
- Use large, readable fonts
- High contrast
- Brief text (viewers can't pause to read while looking around)

---

**In Compositor:**

- Subtle adjustments (VR amplifies effects)

**Considerations:**
- Test on actual VR headset
- Avoid extreme saturation

## Troubleshooting

### Seams visible in render

**Check:**
- Node group settings
- Equirectangular projection is correct
- No manual camera modifications

**Solution:** Recreate rig if needed.

---

### Empty/missing areas in 360¬∞

**Cause:** Not considering all directions

**Solution:**
- Review scene from multiple angles
- Add content in all directions
- Check ceiling and floor

---

### Video not recognized as 360¬∞ on YouTube

**Cause:** Missing metadata

**Solution:**
- Use spatial-media tool
- Inject 360¬∞ metadata
- Verify 2:1 aspect ratio

---

### Motion sickness when viewing

**Causes:**
- Camera movement too fast
- Camera rotation present
- Sudden movements

**Solutions:**
- Minimize camera movement
- Never rotate camera
- Smooth, slow dollies only
- Test on VR headset
