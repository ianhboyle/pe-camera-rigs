# YouTube VR180 Video Specifications

**Research Date:** 2025-12-07
**Target:** Direct-to-YouTube VR180 workflow from Blender

---

## Overview

VR180 is YouTube's stereoscopic 180° VR video format that captures a front-facing 180-degree field of view with dual channels for left and right eyes, providing depth perception in VR headsets.

---

## Hardware Specifications (for reference)

### Camera Requirements
- **IPD (Interpupillary Distance):** 64mm between optical axes (emulates human eye separation)
- **Field of View:** ~100° typical for VR headsets, up to 180° capture
- **Imaging Planes:** Must be aligned and in the same plane
- **Frame Rate:** 60fps minimum (30fps causes motion sickness in VR)
  - VR headset refresh: 60-120fps
  - Higher frame rates critical for smooth motion

### IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)
- High-quality IMU recommended for motion stabilization
- Required for handheld capture (otherwise tripod-only)
- Rotation estimation tolerance: 0.25°
- Critical for reducing motion-induced sickness

---

## Video Format Specifications

### Resolution

**Recommended for VR180 Side-by-Side:**
- **5760×2880** pixels (combined SBS)
  - Left eye: 2880×2880
  - Right eye: 2880×2880
- **Aspect Ratio:** 2:1 (side-by-side combined)
- **Per-Eye Ratio:** 1:1 (square for each eye)

**Alternative Resolutions:**
- 4K: 3840×1920 (1920×1920 per eye)
- 8K: 7680×3840 (3840×3840 per eye)

### Encoding

**Codec:**
- **H.265 (HEVC)** - Required for best results
- Bitrate: **100Mbps** minimum for quality

**Container:**
- MP4 or MOV

**Aspect Ratio:**
- **16:9** or **2:1** for best compatibility
- YouTube optimized for landscape orientation

### Frame Rate
- **Minimum:** 30fps (not recommended for VR)
- **Recommended:** 60fps
- **Optimal:** 90-120fps (matches VR headset refresh rates)

---

## Projection Format

### VR180 Specific
- **Projection Type:** Mesh projection (most generic)
- **Works With:** Fisheye projection (Equisolid recommended)
- **Field of View:** 180° horizontal and vertical
- **Stereo Layout:** Side-by-Side (Left-Right)

### Metadata Requirements

**Spatial Metadata V2:**
- Must inject VR180 metadata before upload
- Camera Motion Metadata Track required
- Includes: camera orientation, gyroscope, accelerometer data

**Tools for Metadata Injection:**
- Google Spatial Media Metadata Injector
- Adobe Premiere (2019 or higher)
- Custom scripts using spatial-media tools

---

## Upload Requirements

### Pre-Upload Processing
1. **Modify file with spatial metadata** using:
   - Adobe Premiere (2019+)
   - Google's spatial-media scripts
   - Other VR180-compatible tools

2. **Processing Time:**
   - Up to 1 hour for 360°/180° playback to be available after upload

### File Size
- Uncompressed renders strongly recommended initially
- Compress with H.265 before upload
- Large files acceptable (YouTube handles up to several GB)

---

## Best Practices for YouTube VR180

### Video Quality
- **Resolution:** Higher is better - 5760×2880 or higher
- **Bitrate:** 100Mbps+ for H.265
- **Color Space:** Rec.709 or Rec.2020
- **Dynamic Range:** SDR standard, HDR supported

### Content Considerations
- **Camera Movement:** Minimal recommended (causes motion sickness)
- **Camera Height:** Eye level (~1.6m / 5'3" - 5'11")
- **Orientation:** Keep camera level (90° angle)
- **Stabilization:** Critical for handheld - use IMU data

### Audio
- **Spatial Audio:** Ambisonic or spatial audio highly recommended
- **Channels:** Stereo minimum, 4-channel+ for spatial
- **Sample Rate:** 48kHz
- **Bitrate:** 384kbps+ for spatial audio

## Workflow Summary

### Blender to YouTube Pipeline

1. **Render in Blender:**
   - Use VR180 fisheye rig (Equisolid, 180° FOV, 64mm IPD)
   - Output: 5760×2880, 60fps, uncompressed or high-quality intermediate

2. **Composite Side-by-Side:**
   - Combine left/right eye renders
   - Output as single SBS video

3. **Encode with H.265:**
   - 100Mbps bitrate
   - MP4 container
   - AAC audio if applicable

4. **Inject Spatial Metadata:**
   - Use Google's spatial-media tools or Adobe Premiere
   - Mark as VR180, stereoscopic, left-right layout

5. **Upload to YouTube:**
   - Standard upload process
   - Wait up to 1 hour for VR processing

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue: Video Not Showing as VR180
- **Solution:** Ensure spatial metadata is injected correctly
- **Check:** File must have VR180 projection metadata before upload

### Issue: Stereo Effect Not Working
- **Solution:** Verify IPD is set to 64mm
- **Check:** Left/right images are properly aligned (same imaging plane)

### Issue: Motion Sickness Reports
- **Solution:** Increase frame rate to 60fps minimum
- **Check:** Camera movement is minimal and smooth
- **Check:** Camera is level and at eye height

### Issue: Low Quality After Upload
- **Solution:** Increase bitrate to 100Mbps+
- **Solution:** Use H.265 codec (not H.264)
- **Wait:** YouTube may still be processing higher quality versions
